## The Problem with Going Too Big Too Fast

The reference site (Shouse Law) has 700+ pages — but they built that over **10+ years** with massive domain authority, a team of writers, and thousands of backlinks. For a newer firm, deploying hundreds of pages at once creates real risks:

### Google's Helpful Content System (HCU)
- Google actively penalizes sites that publish large volumes of template-driven content that exists primarily for search engines rather than users
- Sites that went from 30 pages to 500+ overnight have been hit with ranking drops
- Recovery from an HCU penalty takes 6-12 months minimum
- The signal Google watches: **ratio of helpful vs. thin content**

### Crawl Budget
- Google allocates a limited number of crawls per site based on its authority
- A site with 30 pages suddenly having 700+ means most new pages won't get crawled for weeks/months
- Pages that get crawled but have thin content can hurt the ENTIRE site's rankings

### Content Quality at Scale
- Every page needs genuinely unique, substantive content (not the same template with swapped keywords)
- A bad page doesn't just fail to rank — it dilutes the authority of your good pages
- V2 template pages are ~900 lines each. 700 pages = 630,000 lines. Quality control becomes impossible.

## SEO Safeguards

### What we do to avoid Google penalties:
1. **Gradual rollout** — never more than 30 new pages in a single deploy
2. **Unique content per page** — no boilerplate with keyword swaps
3. **Sitemap management** — update `sitemap.xml` with each batch, submit to Google Search Console
4. **Internal linking** — every new page links to 3-4 related pages on the site (not orphaned)
5. **Canonical URLs** — prevent duplicate content issues
6. **robots.txt** — ensure all new directories are crawlable
7. **Quality threshold** — each V2 page must have: unique statute analysis, original examples, real FAQ, proper schema markup
8. **Monitor in Search Console** — watch for crawl errors, indexing issues, manual actions after each deploy

### What we DON'T do:
- Deploy 700 pages at once
- Use the same template text with only the crime name changed
- Create pages for obscure codes that no one searches for (waste of crawl budget)
- Add "coming soon" placeholder pages (Google sees these as thin content)
