# Legal Code Page Template Structure

## 1. Code and Title

**Format**

[Code Family] § [Code Number] – [Plain English Offense Name]

**Purpose**

Identify the statute clearly and target the primary SEO keyword.

---

## 2. Intro Summary (Plain English Explanation)

Short paragraph explaining:

- What the law prohibits
- Who the law applies to
- Whether it is a misdemeanor / felony / wobbler
- Key penalties

**Template**

[Code section] makes it a crime to [describe illegal conduct in plain English].

A violation of this law is typically charged as a [misdemeanor/felony/wobbler] punishable by:

- [Penalty 1]
- [Penalty 2]
- [Penalty 3]

This law generally applies to [people or professions affected].

---

## 3. Blockquote with Statute Text

Provide the actual law language.

**Template**

[Code section] states:

> "[Insert statute language]"

Purpose:

- Legal authority  
- SEO relevance  
- Trust signal

---

## 4. Applicability Explanation

Explain who the law applies to.

**Template**

This law applies to individuals who are legally authorized to [activity].

Examples include:

- [Profession 1]
- [Profession 2]
- [Profession 3]

---

## 5. Examples Mini-Section

Provide 3–4 real-world scenarios showing how the law may be violated.

**Template**

Examples of violations include:

- [Example scenario 1]
- [Example scenario 2]
- [Example scenario 3]

---

## 6. Defenses Mini-Section (Preview)

List possible legal defenses before the main article sections.

**Template**

Possible defenses to this charge include:

- The conduct was not illegal
- The incident was isolated
- Police misconduct or entrapment

---

## 7. Table of Contents (Anchor Navigation)

Provide clickable internal links to article sections.

**Template**

1. When is it a crime to violate the statute?
2. What legal defenses are available?
   - Defense one
   - Defense two
   - Defense three
3. Penalties and sentencing
4. Related offenses
   - Related offense A
   - Related offense B
   - Related offense C

---

## 8. Section 1 – When Is It a Crime?

Explain the legal rule and elements of the offense.

**Template**

[Code section] makes it illegal to:

- [Legal element 1]
- [Legal element 2]

A violation occurs when [explanation of how courts determine violations].

---

## 9. Section 2 – Legal Defenses

Explain available defenses.

**Template**

Common defenses include:

1. Defense summary  
2. Defense summary  
3. Defense summary  

---

## 10. Defense Subsections

Each defense gets its own subsection.

### 2.1 Defense Name

Explain the defense and provide an example.

### 2.2 Defense Name

Explain the legal reasoning and possible evidence.

### 2.3 Defense Name

Explain how the defense defeats the charge.

---

## 11. Section 3 – Penalties, Punishment, and Sentencing

Explain legal consequences.

**Template**

A violation of [code section] is charged as a [offense level].

Possible penalties include:

- Jail time
- Fines
- Probation
- Other consequences

---

## 12. Section 4 – Related Offenses

List related crimes prosecutors often charge together.

**Template**

Related laws include:

- [Offense 1]
- [Offense 2]
- [Offense 3]

---

## 13. Related Offense Subsections

Each related law gets its own subsection.

**Template**

Explain the law, what conduct violates it, the penalties, and how it differs from the main offense.
