Alameda County Courts & Facilities

Alameda County operates multiple courthouses spread across the county, from downtown Oakland to Fremont. If you have been arrested or must appear in court, here is what you need to know.

Rene C. Davidson Courthouse

1225 Fallon Street, Oakland, CA 94612

The main Alameda County courthouse. Handles felony criminal trials, civil unlimited cases, probate, and complex litigation for the county.

Wiley W. Manuel Courthouse

661 Washington Street, Oakland, CA 94607

Hayward Hall of Justice

24405 Amador Street, Hayward, CA 94544

Fremont Hall of Justice

Downtown Oakland detention facility used for booking and short-term holds following arrests in Oakland and surrounding areas.

Common Legal Issues in Alameda County

Alameda County's diverse communities — from Oakland's urban neighborhoods to the Tri-Valley suburbs — generate a wide range of legal matters. Some of the most common issues we handle include:

  • Gun and weapons charges — Oakland and surrounding cities see a high volume of firearms arrests, with prosecutors seeking enhanced penalties under California's gun laws
  • Violent crime defense — Assault, robbery, and homicide cases are more prevalent in parts of Alameda County than in most Bay Area jurisdictions, and carry severe consequences
  • I-880 and I-580 accidents — These heavily-trafficked freeways connect Oakland to the rest of the East Bay and see frequent multi-vehicle collisions, particularly involving commercial trucks
  • Port and warehouse injuries — The Port of Oakland and surrounding industrial zones employ thousands of workers who face serious injury risks from heavy equipment, forklifts, and loading operations
  • Landlord-tenant disputes — Oakland and Berkeley both have strong rent control ordinances, generating constant litigation over just-cause evictions, rent increases, and habitability complaints
  • Sideshow enforcement — Alameda County law enforcement has increased crackdowns on illegal sideshows, with participants facing vehicle seizure, reckless driving charges, and potential felony prosecution
  • Drug charges — Despite progressive DA policies, drug possession and distribution cases remain heavily prosecuted, particularly cases involving fentanyl and organized distribution networks
