San Francisco Courts & Facilities

As a combined city and county, San Francisco operates a consolidated court system. If you have been arrested or must appear in court, here is what you need to know.

San Francisco Superior Court — Civic Center

400 McAllister Street, San Francisco, CA 94102

The main courthouse for the City and County of San Francisco. Handles civil unlimited and limited cases, family law, probate, and complex litigation. Located in the Civic Center district near City Hall.

Hall of Justice

850 Bryant Street, San Francisco, CA 94103

Handles all criminal matters for San Francisco, including felony and misdemeanor arraignments, preliminary hearings, and trials. Also houses the San Francisco District Attorney's Office, the SFPD Southern Station, and a county jail facility.

San Francisco County Jail — 425 7th Street

425 7th Street, San Francisco, CA 94103

One of the primary booking and detention facilities in San Francisco. If a family member has been arrested in SF, they may be booked here or at the Hall of Justice. Contact us immediately for bail and defense assistance.

San Francisco County Jail — Hall of Justice

850 Bryant Street, San Francisco, CA 94103

Detention facility located within the Hall of Justice complex. Used for booking, short-term holds, and housing detainees with upcoming court appearances.

Common Legal Issues in San Francisco

San Francisco's unique status as a combined city and county, its dense urban environment, and its progressive political landscape create a distinctive set of legal challenges. Some of the most common matters we handle include:

  • Drug possession and fentanyl charges — San Francisco has seen a dramatic increase in drug-related prosecutions, particularly involving fentanyl. The DA's office has shifted toward tougher enforcement in the Tenderloin and SoMa
  • Property crime and retail theft — Theft, burglary, and organized retail theft have been heavily prosecuted in recent years, with enhanced penalties for repeat offenders
  • Rent control disputes — San Francisco's rent control ordinance, administered by the SF Rent Board, generates constant litigation over just-cause evictions, illegal rent increases, buyout agreements, and tenant harassment claims
  • Pedestrian and bicycle accidents — San Francisco's Vision Zero initiative has not eliminated the city's dangerous intersections. Pedestrians and cyclists are struck regularly on Market Street, the Embarcadero, and throughout SoMa and the Tenderloin
  • Muni and transit accidents — Injuries on or caused by Muni buses, light rail, and cable cars involve claims against the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency (SFMTA)
  • Tech employee disputes — Wrongful termination, stock option disputes, non-compete enforcement, and discrimination claims are frequent in San Francisco's tech sector
  • ADA compliance lawsuits — San Francisco businesses face a high volume of Americans with Disabilities Act accessibility lawsuits, particularly targeting restaurants, retail stores, and older commercial buildings
  • Tourist-area robbery and assault — Fisherman's Wharf, Union Square, and other tourist-heavy areas see elevated rates of robbery and assault charges, often involving visitors and local business owners
